ELA Smart Glasses

UX concept supporting focus, accessibility, and individualized learning.

Project Overview

ELA (EduLearning Assistant) is a conceptual prototype for smart glasses designed to support students with ADHD and neurodiverse learning needs. The concept was developed as a part of an inclusive UX design course, with a focus on accessibility, modular interaction, and minimal cognitive load. Features include voice navigation, gesture control, customizable filters, and companion apps for learning environments.

Role

Lead UX Designer & Accessibility Researcher

  • Led research and co-design with students who self-identified as neurodivergent
  • Designed low-fidelity prototypes including props, roleplay scripts, and flipbooks
  • Conducted feedback sessions and iterated based on usability and clarity
  • Created visual brand identity and interaction flows
  • Presented results as a vaporware-style concept to simulate product storytelling

Technology & Methods

  • Sketching, roleplay, and props (paper prototyping)
  • Accessibility heuristics and inclusive design literature
  • Figma (branding and UI components)
  • User testing with feedback matrices
  • Presentation using Framer and Canva
