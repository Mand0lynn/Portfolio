About Me

I was born in the Philippines and raised in a cross-cultural household shaped by Puerto Rican, Filipinx, and Midwestern (via Wisconsin) influences. That upbringing taught me how to navigate complexity, listen deeply, and find common ground—skills that continue to guide my work in tech and systems design.

I spent over 15 years leading strategy and operations at Microsoft and AWS, where I built programs, aligned teams, and solved problems at scale. Along the way, I earned a degree in Integrated Social Sciences from the University of Washington where I explored systems, equity, and the social dimensions of technology.

Today, I’m continuing that work through a focus on cybersecurity, accessibility, and ethical design—building systems that are not only technically sound, but human-centered and built to last.

Future State

I'm currently studying Information Technology with a focus on cybersecurity and information assurance. My coursework spans systems analysis, C#, Java, networking, and technical writing, along with hands-on work in cybersecurity tools and practices. I'm especially interested in the intersection of secure system design, compliance frameworks, and user-centered thinking. With experience in strategy, program delivery, and compliance at Microsoft and AWS, I'm now building the technical foundation to protect systems—and the people who rely on them. My goal is to work in roles where security, ethics, and accessibility all matter.
